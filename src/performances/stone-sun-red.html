Stone, Sun, Red

《石头，太阳，红色》

SAIC Chinese Theatre Club · 芝加哥艺术学院中文剧社

About the Production | 关于本次演出

Set in an age when it has become increasingly difficult to sustain concentration and create, Stone, Sun, Red portrays the condition of individuals constantly interrupted and dispersed under the gaze of others. It places painter Mark Rothko, the "rioters" in Stan Lai's writing, and poet Haizi on the same stage, allowing them to encounter and question one another.

剧目《石头，太阳，红色》以当下这个愈发难以专注创作的时代为背景，呈现个体在他人目光之中不断被打断、被分散的生存处境。作品将画家罗斯科、赖声川笔下的"乱民"以及诗人海子置于同一舞台，使他们彼此相遇、彼此叩问。

A struggle over expression and an inquiry into existence — set between wit and seriousness, attempting to let the viewer experience the "lightness of weight." Between color, language, and silence: how does the individual recognize what is real within the virtual? Can art still become the final way out? 一次关于表达的挣扎，也是一场关于存在的追问，并试图在诙谐与严肃之间，让观者体会到"轻盈的重量"。

Three Figures on One Stage | 三个人物，一个舞台

Mark Rothko
Abstract Expressionist painter, creator of the "Color Field." His work was an attempt to make color itself speak — and a lifelong struggle with whether art can hold meaning.
The "Rioters" from Stan Lai
Characters from Stan Lai's theatrical universe — figures who disrupt order in search of authentic existence, who refuse the scripts others have written for them.
Haizi (海子)
Chinese poet (1964–1989), known for poems of extraordinary clarity and yearning. His writing sought an absolute truth in language — and the question of whether that truth can survive.

About SAIC Chinese Theatre Club

A young and energetic creative group at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago, SAIC Chinese Theatre Club brings together students and practitioners from diverse artistic backgrounds. Compared with more traditional theatre clubs, it places greater emphasis on expanding artistic boundaries and exploring the possibilities between theatre, visual art, installation, and new media.
