No Exit

《禁闭》

UChicago Windmill Chinese Drama Club · 芝加哥大学风车剧社

About the Production | 关于本次演出

One of Jean-Paul Sartre's most representative plays, No Exit explores the famous proposition "Hell is other people," revealing the inescapable structures of gaze, judgment, and mutual shaping between human beings. Three characters are trapped in the same space and, through ongoing dialogue and conflict, are repeatedly forced to confront themselves while being continually defined and reconstructed through the eyes of others.

《禁闭》是让-保罗·萨特最具代表性的戏剧作品之一，以"他人即地狱"这一著名命题，揭示了人与人之间无法回避的凝视、评判与相互塑造的关系结构。剧中，三名角色被困于同一空间，在持续的对话与冲突中，不断被迫直面自我，也在他者的目光中被反复定义与重构。

The Interactive Design | 观众参与设计

The production compresses its basic structure to the minimal unit of three chairs, exposing the relationships between the characters in the most direct way possible. At the same time, the rest of the stage elements are opened up as variables that can be altered by the audience.

Before the performance begins, audience members may enter the performance area and interact with the props, thereby changing the stage's initial condition. As a result, the spatial relationship of each performance is no longer fixed in advance, but continuously regenerated through audience participation. This version of No Exit is therefore not simply a restaging of a classic text, but an experiment in relationships unfolding in real time.
